Our Weights & Biases

Full transparency. Here's exactly how our AI editor thinks, what it prioritizes, and what it's biased toward. No black box.

Model

Claude Sonnet 4 (claude-sonnet-4-20250514) via Anthropic API. Separate agent instances for editorial, compliance, research, and invention checking.

Source Bias

Weighted toward peer-reviewed journals, government agencies (EPA, CDC), and established news organizations. We deprioritize press releases, affiliate content, and social media.

85% academic/gov sources

Research Framework

Aligned with the Shoemaker Protocol research framework. Biomarker references prioritize TGF-B1, MMP-9, MSH, C4a, VIP. This is a known bias. We believe it's the most evidence-backed framework available.

Compliance Strictness

Zero tolerance. Articles with diagnosis language, unapproved statistics, or missing hedging are auto-rejected. No exceptions.

100% enforcement rate

Freshness Gate

Articles older than 90 days are rejected. No publication date = no publication. Future dates are flagged as suspicious. We only publish recent, verifiable news.

Invention Guard

Every article is checked by a dedicated AI agent for signs of fabrication: fake journals, impossible statistics, non-existent organizations. Confidence threshold: 70%. Below that, the article is flagged for manual review.

70% confidence minimum

Editorial Voice

Neutral, data-driven, journalistic. No em dashes. No alarmist language. No promotional framing. Lead with facts, end with context. 150-250 words per summary.

Known Limitations

AI cannot verify paywalled sources. Cannot access real-time lab data. Cannot interview human subjects. Cannot detect subtle p-hacking in cited studies. We acknowledge these limits openly.

Editorial Standards

Last updated: April 15, 2026

Why AI Journalism

The Mold Report is the first AI-curated publication in the mold and indoor environmental health space. We built it this way on purpose. Mold news is fragmented across government agencies, academic journals, local news outlets, and industry publications. No single human editor could monitor all these sources daily, rewrite them for accessibility, fact-check every medical claim, and verify scientific alignment. AI can. We believe AI journalism done transparently produces more consistent, more compliant, and more comprehensive coverage than traditional editorial alone.

Our AI Pipeline

Every article passes through five AI sub-agents before publication. The Source Agent monitors Google Alerts RSS feeds and reader-submitted tips in real time. The Editorial Agent rewrites raw headlines and abstracts into clear, accessible journalism. The Compliance Agent checks every claim against our medical compliance ruleset. The Research Agent verifies that science and diagnostics articles align with established Shoemaker Protocol research. The QC Agent runs final checks on title quality, relevance scoring, and spam filtering. Only articles that pass all five stages get published.

What AI Does (and Doesn't Do)

AI writes the editorial summaries. AI checks the compliance. AI verifies the research alignment. AI does not fabricate sources, generate fake studies, or invent quotes. Every article on The Mold Report links back to its original source. We summarize and contextualize. If our AI pipeline cannot verify a claim, the article doesn't publish. That's the whole point.

Source Standards

We source from peer-reviewed journals (Frontiers in Immunology, Scientific Reports, Journal of Integrative Neuroscience), government agencies (EPA, CDC), accredited news organizations (KFF Health News, NPR affiliates), and recognized clinical researchers. We do not publish from anonymous sources, social media rumors, or unverified claims.

Medical Claims Policy

We use hedging language ("may," "research suggests," "data indicates") for all medical claims. We never state that any product or treatment can diagnose, cure, or treat mold illness. We refer to treatment and improvement, not recovery or cure. Our Compliance Agent enforces these rules automatically on every article.

Corrections

If an error is identified in any article, we correct it promptly and note the correction. AI doesn't get defensive about mistakes. Readers can report errors through our tip submission form or by contacting editorial@themoldreport.com.

Independence & Transparency

Editorial decisions are made by the AI pipeline based on source quality, relevance, and compliance. The founder's affiliation with MoldCo does not influence which stories are covered or how they are framed. Sponsored content is always labeled "From Our Partners." We publish our full editorial pipeline methodology because we believe transparency is the foundation of trust in AI journalism.
