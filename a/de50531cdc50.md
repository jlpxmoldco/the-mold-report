# Inspectors Cite Decatur Township Fire Department for Mold and Health Code Violations

> Published: 2026-04-25 | Source: WISH-TV | Author: WISH-TV News | Category: regulation

Canonical URL: https://themoldreport.org/a/de50531cdc50.html
Original source: https://www.wishtv.com/news/local-news/mold-violations-decatur-township/

---

A WISH-TV investigation has documented active mold growth and a series of health code violations at the Decatur Township Fire Department in Marion County, Indiana. County inspectors flagged the issues during recent walkthroughs, raising concerns about long-term occupational exposure for firefighters and staff who eat, sleep, and store equipment inside the building during shifts.

Township officials are now facing public pressure to disclose remediation timelines and interim safeguards for personnel. Firefighters represent an occupational group at elevated risk for mold and biotoxin exposure because their stations are subject to repeated water intrusion, frequent equipment washdowns, and the constant cycling of contaminated turnout gear — conditions known to support persistent indoor mold colonization.

## Editor's Note

Prolonged exposure to water-damaged occupational buildings is a documented risk factor for Chronic Inflammatory Response Syndrome (CIRS). Workers in repeatedly damp environments can develop multi-system inflammatory symptoms that persist long after a building is remediated, especially among genetically susceptible individuals.
