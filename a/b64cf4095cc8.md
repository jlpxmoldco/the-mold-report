# Ohio State Mold Lawsuit Shifts Focus to Student Medical Records Dispute

A mold-related lawsuit against Ohio State University has taken an unexpected legal turn, with the dispute now centering on access to students' medical records, according to local news reports. The case likely involves students or former students who claim mold exposure in campus housing or facilities caused health problems, and who have filed suit against the university. The new development suggests either the university is seeking access to plaintiffs' medical histories to challenge the extent of their claimed injuries, or conversely, plaintiffs are fighting to obtain university health records relevant to their cases. Disputes over medical records in mold litigation are common, as establishing a direct link between exposure and illness requires substantial documentation. The outcome of this records battle could significantly shape the trajectory of the broader case, determining what evidence is admissible and how liability might ultimately be assessed. Mold-related illness cases on college campuses have grown more frequent in recent years as students and advocacy groups push institutions to address aging building infrastructure and moisture control failures.
