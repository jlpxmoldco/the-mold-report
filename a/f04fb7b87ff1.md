# Renters Report Mold and Cold in Sam Stair's S2 Rental Properties

An AOL report documents complaints from renters who say they live with mold, cold, and unsafe conditions in properties owned by Sam Stair's S2 portfolio. Tenants describe persistent mold growth alongside other habitability problems, raising questions about landlord accountability and enforcement of housing codes that govern indoor mold.

Mold growth in rental housing is a recurring source of tenant disputes across the United States. Persistent moisture from leaks, inadequate ventilation, and deferred maintenance are common contributors, and chronic indoor mold exposure is associated with respiratory symptoms, allergic reactions, and other health complaints.

For affected tenants, documenting conditions in writing, filing formal complaints with local code enforcement, and reviewing state-specific landlord-tenant law are typical first steps. Some jurisdictions impose specific mold-disclosure or remediation obligations on landlords.
