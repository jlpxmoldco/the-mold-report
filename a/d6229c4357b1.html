Michigan Prisoner Says Mold Exposure Is Killing Her, Pleads for Governor's Help

Source: Detroit Metro Times 2026-01-05 3

A Michigan inmate is reportedly suffering from a serious mold-related illness and has made a direct appeal to Governor Gretchen Whitmer, asking for intervention that she says could save her life. Reports indicate the woman believes her condition stems from mold exposure within the correctional facility where she is housed. The case draws attention to ongoing concerns about air quality and environmental health conditions inside Michigan's prison system, where inmates have limited ability to remove themselves from harmful environments. Mold exposure in institutional settings can be particularly dangerous for individuals with genetic susceptibility to mold-related illness, as research suggests their immune systems may struggle to clear biotoxins that others process without symptoms. The inmate's appeal to the governor suggests she has likely exhausted other avenues for relief, including requests through prison medical staff or facility administrators. As of the report's publication, it was not clear whether Whitmer's office had responded. The case may add pressure on state officials to inspect and remediate mold conditions in Michigan correctional facilities.
