# Cleveland Healthcare Facility Fails Mold Inspection Following Nurse Complaints

> Published: 2026-01-16 | Source: Cleveland 19 News | Author: Local News | Category: news

Canonical URL: https://themoldreport.org/a/6b624cae7194.html
Original source: https://www.cleveland19.com/2026/01/15/cleveland-healthcare-facility-fails-mold-inspection-after-nurse-complaints/

---

A Cleveland healthcare facility has failed a mold inspection after nursing staff raised concerns about potential mold contamination on the premises, according to local news reports. The complaints, filed by nurses working in the facility, appear to have triggered an official inspection that uncovered conditions serious enough to result in a formal failure. Reports indicate the mold was discovered in areas of the building accessible to both staff and potentially patients. Mold problems in healthcare settings carry heightened concern because immunocompromised patients face greater health risks from exposure. The facility now faces pressure to remediate the contamination before resuming normal operations or passing a follow-up inspection. Nurse-driven complaints have increasingly become a key mechanism for surfacing indoor air quality problems in medical buildings, where routine inspections may not catch slow-developing mold growth. The incident raises broader questions about inspection frequency standards for healthcare facilities and whether existing protocols are sufficient to protect both workers and patients from mold-related illness.
