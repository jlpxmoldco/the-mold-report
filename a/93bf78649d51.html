Hinesville Mother Diagnosed with Respiratory Illness Amid Apartment Mold Complaints

Source: wtoc.com 2026-04-30 3 min read

A pregnant resident of Hinesville, Georgia, says she was diagnosed with a respiratory illness at Liberty Regional Medical Center last week and believes mold inside her apartment is to blame. The tenant, identified as McGee, had been raising concerns about visible mold in the unit before her diagnosis.

After local media coverage, the apartment management offered a solution to address the contamination. The case echoes a recurring pattern documented across U.S. multi-family housing, where tenants — especially pregnant women, infants, and the immunocompromised — shoulder the health consequences of building moisture problems before remediation occurs.

Editor's Note: For pregnant residents, prompt removal from a water-damaged building is the first step in any mold-related care plan. Cases like this are the textbook scenario where Shoemaker-trained clinicians recommend documented relocation alongside biomarker workup.
