# NC Supreme Court Bars Mold Damage Claims Citing Contract Liability Limits

> Published: 2026-03-23 | Source: Law360 | Author: Court Records / Local News | Category: regulation

Canonical URL: https://themoldreport.org/a/1ea59ddb0ae0.html
Original source: https://www.law360.com/insurance/articles/2455848

---

The North Carolina Supreme Court has ruled against plaintiffs seeking damages related to mold exposure, upholding contractual liability limitations that effectively blocked their claims. Reports indicate the case centered on whether standard contract language could be used to cap or eliminate compensation for mold-related harm, a question with broad implications for property owners, tenants, and contractors across the state. The court sided with defendants, ruling that the liability limits in the underlying contract were enforceable. Legal observers note the decision may make it significantly harder for North Carolina residents to pursue mold damage claims when contracts contain such limiting language. The ruling could affect how property management agreements, construction contracts, and lease agreements are written and contested going forward. Tenant advocacy groups and plaintiffs' attorneys are expected to push for legislative remedies to address the gap in consumer protections this decision creates. The case underscores the importance of carefully reviewing contract terms before signing, particularly clauses that restrict liability for environmental hazards like mold.
