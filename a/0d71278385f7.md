# New Study: MARCoNS Bacteria Directly Suppress Alpha-MSH, Confirming Key Step in Shoemaker's Biotoxin Pathway

> Published: 2026-04-22 | Source: Frontiers in Endocrinology | Category: research

Canonical URL: https://themoldreport.org/a/0d71278385f7.html
Original source: https://doi.org/10.3389/fendo.2026.1728408

---

A new peer-reviewed study published in Frontiers in Endocrinology provides the first direct evidence that MARCoNS (Multiple Antibiotic Resistant Coagulase Negative Staphylococci) bacteria suppress alpha-MSH production through specific enzymatic degradation. The research identifies the molecular mechanism by which these nasal biofilm-forming bacteria break down alpha-MSH, a key regulatory peptide in the hypothalamic-pituitary axis. This finding validates a critical node in Dr. Ritchie Shoemaker's biotoxin pathway model, which has long proposed that MARCoNS colonization in mold-exposed patients leads to MSH suppression, driving downstream dysregulation of cytokines, hormones, and immune function. The study's authors note that this mechanism may explain why MARCoNS-positive patients often present with treatment-resistant symptoms including fatigue, cognitive impairment, and hormonal imbalances.

## Editor's Note

This study provides direct molecular evidence for a mechanism long proposed by Dr. Ritchie Shoemaker — that MARCoNS bacteria in the nasal passages of mold-exposed patients actively degrade alpha-MSH, a master regulatory peptide. In the Shoemaker biotoxin pathway, MSH suppression is a critical downstream effect of biotoxin exposure in genetically susceptible individuals (HLA-DR positive), leading to dysregulated cytokines (TGF-β1, MMP-9, C4a), hormonal disruption, and chronic inflammation. This is the first peer-reviewed confirmation of the specific enzymatic pathway by which MARCoNS achieve this suppression. For patients currently in the Shoemaker Protocol, this research reinforces why MARCoNS eradication (typically via BEG spray) is an essential early step before VIP nasal spray can be effective.
