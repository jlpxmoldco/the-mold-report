Mycotoxin Testing Market Set to Nearly Double by 2034, Hitting $2.78 Billion

Source: Fortune Business Insights 2026-04-25 3

The global mycotoxin testing market is projected to grow from $1.53 billion in 2026 to $2.78 billion by 2034 — a compound annual growth rate of 7.73% — according to industry forecasts. The figures cover the full breadth of mycotoxin assay use, including food and feed safety, agricultural trade, and clinical diagnostics, with growth driven by tightening regulatory frameworks and rising awareness of indoor mold exposure.

Within the clinical segment, demand is being shaped by direct-to-consumer expansion. RealTime Laboratories announced in January 2026 that its 16-mycotoxin at-home panel was now available for direct consumer purchase. Mosaic Diagnostics — formerly Great Plains Laboratory — continues to run its 11-mycotoxin MycoTOX Profile through practitioner channels. The two competing approaches show the segment is mature enough to support both gated and open-access distribution.

Where food and feed testing is anchored by regulatory mandates such as FDA action levels and EU maximum allowable concentrations, clinical testing remains a more contested space. Major medical bodies have not formally endorsed urinary mycotoxin testing as a standalone diagnostic for human exposure, though it is widely used by integrative and functional medicine practitioners working with chronic mold-illness patients.

Editor's Note: The clinical mycotoxin testing segment is growing faster than its evidence base. Patients should treat any single biomarker as one data point, not a diagnosis — particularly when deciding whether removing exposure from a water-damaged building will resolve symptoms.
