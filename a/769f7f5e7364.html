Michigan Prison Inmate Claims Mold Is Making Her Sick. Lawmakers Push Governor

Source: Clickondetroit 2026-04-18 3

Krystal Clark, an inmate at Huron Valley Correctional Facility in Michigan, has reported that mold exposure inside the prison is causing serious health problems after 16 years of incarceration. Clark's claims have prompted state lawmakers to pressure Governor Gretchen Whitmer's administration to address facility conditions. The case highlights broader concerns about indoor air quality and mold contamination in Michigan's correctional system. Prison conditions, including moisture control and ventilation, have come under scrutiny as potential public health issues affecting both inmates and staff. The incident reflects growing awareness of mold-related health complaints in institutional settings, though specific medical documentation and testing results were not detailed in available reports. This case matters because it raises questions about environmental health standards in correctional facilities, where vulnerable populations spend extended periods in potentially contaminated spaces with limited control over their surroundings.
