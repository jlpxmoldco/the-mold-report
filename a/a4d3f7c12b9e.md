# Psychology Today Spotlights Mold Toxicity as a Driver of Co-Occurring Psychiatric Symptoms

> Published: 2026-05-04 | Source: Psychology Today | Category: news

A new Psychology Today column examines why depression, anxiety, brain fog, and insomnia so often appear together in the same patient, and points to mold toxicity as one underrecognized common cause.

The piece argues that overlapping psychiatric presentations frequently share a single inflammatory driver, and asks clinicians to consider biotoxin exposure when patients present with multi-symptom psychiatric pictures that resist standard treatment.

The column joins a growing wave of mainstream coverage acknowledging that environmental exposures, particularly from water-damaged buildings, can produce neuroinflammatory effects that mimic or worsen primary psychiatric disorders.

## Editor's Note

The neuropsychiatric symptoms of CIRS, fatigue, brain fog, mood changes, sleep disturbance, are well-documented in Dr. Ritchie Shoemaker's published research and reflect the inflammatory cascade triggered by biotoxin exposure in HLA-DR susceptible individuals. Mainstream psychiatric coverage acknowledging this link is a meaningful step.
