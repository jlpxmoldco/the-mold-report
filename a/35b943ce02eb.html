Reality Star's CIRS Diagnosis Puts Mold Illness in the National Spotlight

Source: thelooker.thedailybeast.com 2026-04-24 4

Mikayla Matthews, 26, star of Hulu's The Secret Lives of Mormon Wives, has publicly shared her struggle with symptoms consistent with Chronic Inflammatory Response Syndrome following suspected mold exposure in her Utah home. Matthews reports skin inflammation, neurological issues, hair loss, digestive problems, and hormonal disruptions — a symptom cluster familiar to mold illness researchers.

Notably, Matthews experienced significant symptom relief while traveling to California and Costa Rica, with symptoms returning within two days of coming home to Utah — a pattern that researchers guided by the Shoemaker Protocol recognize as a hallmark of biotoxin-related illness tied to water-damaged buildings.

Editor's Note: While CIRS is not yet recognized as an official diagnosis by all medical bodies, the symptom pattern Matthews describes — multi-system inflammation triggered by environmental exposure and resolving with removal from the exposure source — aligns with over 30 years of peer-reviewed research on innate immune responses to biotoxins. Blood biomarkers such as TGF-β1, MMP-9, and MSH may help objectively identify this type of immune dysregulation.
