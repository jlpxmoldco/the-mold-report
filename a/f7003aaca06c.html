Georgia Tenant Forced to Live in Tent After Landlord Ignores Mold Crisis

Source: MSN 2026-04-24 3 min read

A Georgia woman says she has been forced to live in a tent outside her rental home after persistent mold problems went unaddressed by her landlord, highlighting the intersection of housing negligence and environmental health that affects renters across the country. The woman accuses her landlord of extreme negligence in failing to remediate mold conditions that made the property uninhabitable.

Her situation is far from unique. Renters in water-damaged properties often face a difficult choice between continued exposure and displacement, with limited legal recourse in many states. Unlike homeowners, tenants typically cannot authorize remediation themselves and depend entirely on landlord responsiveness.

Editor's Note: Prolonged mold exposure in residential settings is a well-documented trigger for chronic inflammatory responses. Research guided by the Shoemaker Protocol has shown that occupants of water-damaged buildings may develop multi-system symptoms including fatigue, cognitive difficulty, and respiratory issues. Approximately 50% of residential buildings have some history of water damage, making this a widespread public health concern that disproportionately impacts renters.
