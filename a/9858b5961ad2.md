# Housing Advocate Recounts How Mold Exposure Drove Her Into the Tenant-Justice Movement

> Published: 2026-05-06 | Source: National Low Income Housing Coalition | Author: NLIHC | Category: news

Canonical URL: https://themoldreport.org/a/9858b5961ad2.html
Original source: https://nlihc.org/resource/17-1-overcoming-systemic-barriers

---

In a personal essay published by the National Low Income Housing Coalition, a tenant advocate describes how mold exposure in a Florida apartment was the catalyst that pulled her into housing-justice work. The piece, part of NLIHC's "Overcoming Systemic Barriers" series, frames mold not as an isolated maintenance issue but as a marker of the deeper systemic failures that make safe, healthy housing inaccessible for low-income renters.

The essay adds another data point to a growing body of advocacy connecting indoor environmental quality to tenant rights, fair housing enforcement and landlord accountability — particularly in regions with humid climates where moisture intrusion and water-damaged buildings are common.

NLIHC is one of the most established voices in the affordable-housing policy space, and its decision to platform mold-driven advocacy reflects rising recognition that building health is a housing-justice issue, not just a building-science one.

## Editor's Note

First-person accounts of how mold exposure reshaped a person's life are common in the patient and tenant-advocacy communities, but they rarely surface in mainstream housing policy publications. When they do, it widens the audience that takes water-damaged buildings seriously as a health and rights issue.
