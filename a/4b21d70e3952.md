# Michigan Inmates Sick From Prison Mold Seek Gov. Whitmer's Intervention

> Published: 2026-04-19 | Source: Aol | Category: news

Canonical URL: https://themoldreport.org/a/4b21d70e3952.html
Original source: https://www.aol.com/news/advocates-huron-valley-correctional-facility-012525226.html

---

Incarcerated women at a Michigan state prison are reporting illness they attribute to mold conditions within the facility, prompting calls for Governor Gretchen Whitmer to intervene. The inmates, housed at a women's correctional institution, are seeking direct action from the governor's office to address what they describe as ongoing exposure to mold in their living environment.

The available source details are limited, but the situation reflects a broader pattern of mold-related health complaints emerging from institutional settings, including prisons, schools, and government-owned buildings. Incarcerated populations face compounded risk because they cannot relocate away from a water-damaged building, and institutional maintenance backlogs often allow moisture problems to persist for extended periods before remediation occurs.

For people concerned about mold exposure, this case underscores the intersection of indoor environmental quality and institutional accountability. When occupants have no ability to leave a building voluntarily, the responsibility for maintaining safe air quality falls entirely on the managing authority. Whether or not a formal health investigation follows, the inmates' appeal to the governor signals that standard facility response channels have not resolved the concern.
