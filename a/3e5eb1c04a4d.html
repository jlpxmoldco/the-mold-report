RealTime Laboratories Opens At-Home Mycotoxin Panel to Direct Consumer Sales

Source: RealTime Laboratories 2026-04-25 3

RealTime Laboratories has begun selling its at-home mycotoxin testing panel directly to consumers, the company announced on January 8, 2026. The Texas-based diagnostic lab had previously offered the test only through licensed practitioners, and the move removes one of the longer-standing barriers to mold-illness testing — having to find a clinician familiar with the condition before a sample can even be collected.

The panel uses a noninvasive urine sample to identify 16 clinically relevant mycotoxins, the toxic compounds produced by certain mold species growing in water-damaged buildings and on contaminated food. RealTime's panel covers the families of compounds that have drawn the most clinical attention in CIRS work, including aflatoxins, ochratoxins, trichothecenes, and zearalenones.

The shift mirrors a broader trend in environmental-illness diagnostics. Competitors including Mosaic Diagnostics — formerly Great Plains Laboratory — continue to run comparable 11-mycotoxin urinary panels through practitioner channels. With one major lab now selling direct and another holding the practitioner-referral model, the segment now supports both distribution paths simultaneously.

Editor's Note: Mycotoxin urine testing remains debated in the broader medical community, partly because of unresolved questions about whether a positive result reflects current exposure or stored body burden. Patients pursuing the Shoemaker Protocol typically rely on blood biomarkers — TGF-β1, MMP-9, C4a, VEGF — as the more validated diagnostic pathway.
