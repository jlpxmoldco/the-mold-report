Temple Residents Report Months of Illness Linked to Apartment Mold

Source: KCEN-TV 2026-03-12 3 min read

Tenants at a residential property in Temple say they have been living with mold contamination for several months and that the conditions have affected their health. Reports indicate that residents raised concerns with property management but allege that adequate remediation was not completed in a timely manner. Mold exposure in residential settings is a recognized public health concern, and prolonged exposure may contribute to respiratory symptoms, fatigue, and other health issues, particularly in individuals with certain genetic susceptibilities. Local news coverage suggests the tenants may be pursuing action through housing authorities or legal channels to compel repairs. Stories like this reflect a broader pattern of tenant-landlord disputes over indoor air quality, especially in older housing stock where moisture control can be inadequate. Renters in affected units are generally advised to document conditions thoroughly, report issues in writing to landlords, and consult a physician if they are experiencing symptoms they believe may be related to mold exposure in their homes.
